# Non-Goals: academyinfo-mcp

The integrated release is deliberately narrow. None of the following may be inferred from local implementation or release evidence.

## Data access and mutation

- No website scraping, live OpenAPI bridge, runtime network request, account, or service-key requirement.
- No write-capable MCP tool, source-file mutation, official-portal writeback, external submission, or correction of official values.
- No loss of raw row/cell evidence and no inferred missing value, unit, source column, year, institution, endpoint, link, operation, parameter, response field, header, or marker.
- No raw workbook or signed download URL in package, receipt, or writer output.

## Dataset boundary

- No `15139279` raw, normalized, seed, sample, fixture, SQLite, CSV, JSON, or derived artifact.
- No default metric sourced from that dataset and no granular, per-department, or health-insurance-linked employment feature.
- No source-derived executable TypeScript/JavaScript catalog and no mixing of MIT code-license terms with KOGL data-license obligations.

## Product behavior

- No recommendation, preference inference, weighting, score, rank, best/worst, winner/loser, official evaluation, or user-choice substitution.
- No metric-value sorting of ambiguity candidates and no guessed institution or campus.
- No partial metrics, comparisons, or explanations when any `explore_universities` query is unresolved.
- No removal, rename, or breaking input/response change to the seven legacy tools.
- No official-portal, affiliation, sponsorship, approval, endorsement, or university-representative claim.

## Refresh shortcuts

- No fixed 24-column, fixed row-count, fixed institution-set, previous-value equality, or missing-rate approval gate.
- No prior-checksum-match approval, changed-checksum rejection, or claim that SHA-256 proves official origin/authenticity.
- No rounding or coercion of signs, exponent notation, malformed grouping, Unicode digits, NaN/infinity, precision-losing decimals, or values outside the exact grammar.
- No automatic human approval, writer execution of candidate artifacts, privilege combination, mutable receipt join, or refresh writer invocation on failure/no-change.

## Runtime and support claims

- No Node 24+ or outside-matrix support guarantee. The only intended public matrix is Node 22 macOS/arm64, Windows/x64, and Ubuntu glibc/x64 after public proof.
- No claim that a local install or tarball proves public `npx`, no-compile behavior, registry identity/integrity, provenance, or client compatibility.
- No dual SQLite backends and no automatic fallback. `sql.js` cannot ship without its reviewed Architect+administrator selection receipt.
- No telemetry, runtime update check, or stdout diagnostics in stdio mode.

## Release shortcuts and overclaims

- No assumed public SemVer, npm package ownership, publication approval, candidate completion, `latest` promotion, or rollback readiness.
- No direct publish to `latest`; no product-only or data-only partial release.
- No substitution of generic stdio/configuration evidence for actual Claude Desktop/macOS candidate evidence.
- No claim that Cursor or Codex was tested merely because configuration examples exist.
- No overwrite of an existing npm version and no reuse of a bad version after rollback.
- No closure of a changed-data freshness incident before its matching release-data digest reaches `latest`; no deadline reset after drift or rollback.

## Confidentiality

No credential, service key, publish token, signed query string, private filesystem path, local user name, machine identifier, or unredacted secret may appear in logs, MCP responses, manifests, documentation examples, package artifacts, snapshots, errors, or receipts.
